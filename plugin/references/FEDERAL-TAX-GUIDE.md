## Investment Tax Credit (ITC) & Production Tax Credit (PTC)

Tax credits for on-site renewable energy and energy storage systems at data center facilities. These credits directly offset federal tax liability.

**Post-OBBBA (effective July 4, 2025):**

### Investment Tax Credit (ITC) -- Sections 48 / 48E

The ITC provides a percentage-based credit on the cost of qualifying energy property placed in service.

| Component | Rate | Adder | Total Possible | Qualifying Equipment (DC Application) | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|------|-------|---------------|--------------------------------------|------------|--------|
| Base credit | 6% (or 30% with prevailing wage + apprenticeship) | -- | 6-30% | Solar PV, BESS, fuel cells, combined heat and power, geothermal | confirmed | [IRS Section 48](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/businesses/investment-credit) |
| Domestic content bonus | +10% | Meets domestic content requirements (steel, iron, manufactured products) | Up to 40% | Same as above; must meet percentage thresholds for US-manufactured components | confirmed | [IRS Notice 2024-15](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-drop/n-24-15.pdf) |
| Energy community bonus | +10% | Located in energy community (brownfield, fossil fuel employment, retired coal) | Up to 50% | Same as above; based on DC site location in qualifying census tract | confirmed | [IRS Energy Community Resources](https://energycommunities.gov/) |

**Safe harbor deadline:** Begin construction by July 4, 2026. Property must be placed in service by December 31, 2027. "Begin construction" satisfied by either (1) Physical Work Test or (2) Five Percent Safe Harbor. Confidence: confirmed. Source: [Sidley Austin OBBBA Analysis](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/07/the-one-big-beautiful-bill-act-navigating-the-new-energy-landscape)

### Production Tax Credit (PTC) -- Sections 45 / 45Y

The PTC provides a per-kWh credit for electricity generated by qualifying renewable energy facilities.

| Component | Rate (2026 est.) | Duration | Qualifying Source (DC Application) | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|-----------------------------------|------------|--------|
| Base credit | ~0.3 cents/kWh (or ~2.9 cents/kWh with prevailing wage + apprenticeship) | 10 years from placed in service | On-site or dedicated wind, geothermal, closed-loop biomass | confirmed | [IRS Section 45](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/businesses/renewable-electricity-production-credit) |
| Domestic content bonus | +10% of credit amount | Same | Same; domestic content requirements for components | confirmed | [IRS Notice 2024-15](https://www.irs.gov/pub/irs-drop/n-24-15.pdf) |
| Energy community bonus | +10% of credit amount | Same | Same; site in qualifying energy community | confirmed | [IRS Energy Community Resources](https://energycommunities.gov/) |

**ITC vs PTC election:** Taxpayers elect ITC or PTC per facility (cannot claim both). ITC generally favors high-cost, lower-capacity-factor systems (solar + BESS). PTC generally favors high-capacity-factor generation (wind, geothermal). For DC applications, ITC on solar + BESS is the most common structure. Confidence: confirmed.

**Pre-OBBBA (for reference):**
- ITC and PTC technology-neutral credits (Sections 48E/45Y) were scheduled to phase down starting when greenhouse gas emission targets were met. Post-OBBBA, safe harbor deadline of July 4, 2026 replaces the emission-based phasedown.
- IRA (2022) originally established the prevailing wage + apprenticeship framework for the 30% rate, which remains in effect post-OBBBA.

## MACRS & Bonus Depreciation

Modified Accelerated Cost Recovery System (MACRS) depreciation and bonus depreciation for data center assets. Enables accelerated tax deductions for capital investment.

**Post-OBBBA (effective July 4, 2025):**

100% bonus depreciation restored permanently for qualifying property placed in service on or after January 20, 2025. This applies to MACRS property with a recovery period of 20 years or less, including used property (not just new). This is one of the most significant tax benefits for data center construction.

| Asset Class | MACRS Period | Bonus Depreciation (Post-OBBBA) | DC Examples | Confidence | Source |
|-------------|-------------|--------------------------------|-------------|------------|--------|
| Computer equipment (IRS Asset Class 00.12) | 5 years | 100% (permanent) | Servers, storage, networking, UPS systems | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |
| Office furniture and fixtures (00.11) | 7 years | 100% (permanent) | Network operations center furniture, monitoring displays | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |
| Electrical distribution (49.14) | 15 years | 100% (permanent) | Switchgear, transformers, PDUs, busway, cabling infrastructure | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |
| Land improvements | 15 years | 100% (permanent) | Site work, parking, fencing, landscaping, stormwater management | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |
| Nonresidential real property | 39 years | Not eligible for bonus | Building shell, structural components | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |
| Qualified improvement property (QIP) | 15 years | 100% (permanent) | Interior improvements to existing DC buildings | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |
| Generators and emergency power | 15 years | 100% (permanent) | Diesel/gas generators, ATS, fuel systems | confirmed | [IRS Asset Class 00.4 / 49.14](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |
| HVAC and cooling systems | 15 years (if separate from building) | 100% (if properly classified as personal property via cost segregation) | Chillers, CRAHs, CRACs, cooling towers, CDUs, immersion tanks | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Fire suppression | 7 years (if separate from building) | 100% (if properly classified) | Clean agent systems, sprinklers, VESDA | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Security systems | 5 years | 100% (permanent) | Access control, CCTV, intrusion detection, biometrics | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |

**Pre-OBBBA (for reference):**
- 2025 (pre-July 4): 40% bonus depreciation (phasedown from 100% in 2022)
- 2026: Would have been 20% bonus depreciation
- 2027+: Would have been 0% bonus depreciation (full MACRS schedules only)
- The OBBBA retroactively restored 100% for property placed in service on or after January 20, 2025

> Source: [Sidley Austin OBBBA Analysis](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/07/the-one-big-beautiful-bill-act-navigating-the-new-energy-landscape) | Confidence: confirmed

## Section 179D Energy Efficient Buildings

**URGENT DEADLINE: Section 179D sunsets June 30, 2026. Construction must BEGIN by this date to qualify.**

The Section 179D deduction allows a tax deduction for energy-efficient improvements to commercial buildings, including data centers.

**Post-OBBBA (effective July 4, 2025):**

| Parameter | Value | Notes | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|-------|-------|------------|--------|
| Maximum deduction | $5.36/sqft (2026 inflation-adjusted) | With prevailing wage + apprenticeship; $1.07/sqft base rate | confirmed | [IRS Section 179D](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/energy-efficient-commercial-buildings-deduction) |
| Qualifying standard | 25% improvement over ASHRAE 90.1-2007 reference building | Measured by DOE-qualified software | confirmed | [DOE 179D Page](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/buildings/179d-energy-efficient-commercial-buildings-tax-deduction) |
| Qualifying systems | HVAC, interior lighting, building envelope | Each system can qualify independently or combined | confirmed | [IRS Section 179D](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/energy-efficient-commercial-buildings-deduction) |
| Sunset date | June 30, 2026 | Construction must begin by this date; in-service deadline is Dec 31, 2027 | confirmed | [Sidley Austin OBBBA Analysis](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/07/the-one-big-beautiful-bill-act-navigating-the-new-energy-landscape) |
| Annual recapture | Partial deduction available for partial qualification | Proportional to energy savings achieved | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |

**DC-Specific Application:**
- **HVAC/Cooling:** Most DC cooling systems (chillers, economizers, adiabatic cooling) can qualify if they achieve 25% improvement over ASHRAE 90.1-2007 reference. Given that modern DC cooling far exceeds 2007 standards, most new DC construction qualifies.
- **Lighting:** LED lighting with automated controls in office areas, common spaces, and mechanical rooms. Data halls typically have minimal lighting but support areas contribute.
- **Building envelope:** Insulated panels, high-performance glazing in office areas, thermal barriers. DC shells are typically well-insulated by design.
- **Combined approach:** Most DC projects claim the full $5.36/sqft by combining HVAC + lighting + envelope improvements.

**Pre-OBBBA (for reference):**
- Section 179D was permanent under the IRA (2022)
- OBBBA changed 179D to sunset on June 30, 2026 -- a significant reduction in the incentive window
- Projects that began construction before July 4, 2025 under the permanent provision retain their eligibility under transitional rules

> Source: [IRS Section 179D Guidance](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/energy-efficient-commercial-buildings-deduction), [DOE 179D Resources](https://www.energy.gov/cmei/buildings/179d-energy-efficient-commercial-buildings-tax-deduction) | Confidence: confirmed

## Opportunity Zones

Tax incentive for investment in designated low-income communities. Post-OBBBA, the program was significantly expanded and extended.

**Post-OBBBA (effective July 4, 2025) -- "OZ 2.0":**

| Parameter | Value | Notes | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|-------|-------|------------|--------|
| Program duration | Extended indefinitely | Originally set to expire Dec 31, 2026; OBBBA removed sunset | confirmed | [Novogradac OZ Analysis](https://www.novoco.com/resource-centers/opportunity-zone-resource-center) |
| Deferral mechanism | Rolling 5-year deferral on capital gains | Invest capital gains into Qualified Opportunity Fund (QOF); defer tax for 5 years | confirmed | [IRS Opportunity Zones FAQ](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/opportunity-zones-frequently-asked-questions) |
| Basis step-up | 10% at 5 years; 15% at 7 years | Reduces taxable gain when deferral period ends | confirmed | [Novogradac OZ Analysis](https://www.novoco.com/resource-centers/opportunity-zone-resource-center) |
| Gain exclusion | 100% exclusion of appreciation on QOF investment held 10+ years | Gain on the QOF investment itself (not the deferred gain) is excluded from tax | confirmed | [IRS Opportunity Zones FAQ](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/opportunity-zones-frequently-asked-questions) |
| Post-Dec 31, 2026 investments | New investments get rolling 5-year deferral | No longer tied to original 2017 designation timeline | confirmed | [Sidley Austin OBBBA Analysis](https://www.sidley.com/en/insights/newsupdates/2025/07/the-one-big-beautiful-bill-act-navigating-the-new-energy-landscape) |
| Zone designations | Original 8,764 census tracts + potential new designations | OBBBA authorized new zone designations by governors | unconfirmed (new designations pending) | [CDFI Fund OZ Resources](https://www.cdfifund.gov/opportunity-zones) |

**DC-Specific Application:**
- **Site selection:** Data center developers can deliberately select sites in designated Opportunity Zones to access these benefits. OZ-eligible sites exist in many DC markets (Northern Virginia, Texas, Georgia, Nevada, Ohio).
- **QOF structure:** DC developer forms a QOF, invests capital gains, and uses proceeds to build or acquire DC in an OZ. The 10-year gain exclusion is particularly valuable for DC projects with long hold periods.
- **Stacking:** OZ benefits can be combined with ITC (on-site solar/BESS), bonus depreciation, cost segregation, and state incentives for substantial tax benefit stacking.
- **Caution:** QOF must hold at least 90% of assets in OZ property. Substantial improvement requirement: QOF must invest an amount equal to the adjusted basis of purchased property within 30 months. New construction satisfies this automatically.

**Pre-OBBBA (for reference):**
- Original OZ program (Tax Cuts and Jobs Act 2017) had a deferral deadline of Dec 31, 2026
- Basis step-up was 10% at 5 years, 15% at 7 years (same structure, but calendar deadlines made 7-year step-up impossible for post-2019 investments)
- OBBBA removed all calendar deadlines and replaced with rolling holding periods

> Source: [IRS Opportunity Zones](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/opportunity-zones-frequently-asked-questions), [Novogradac](https://www.novoco.com/resource-centers/opportunity-zone-resource-center) | Confidence: confirmed

## Cost Segregation

Engineering-based study that reclassifies building components into shorter MACRS recovery periods, accelerating depreciation deductions. Combined with 100% bonus depreciation (post-OBBBA), cost segregation enables immediate expensing of a significant portion of DC construction cost.

**Post-OBBBA (effective July 4, 2025):**

With 100% bonus depreciation restored permanently, cost segregation studies for data centers deliver maximum value by identifying assets that can be immediately expensed rather than depreciated over 39 years.

| Component | Standard Classification | Reclassified MACRS Period | Typical % of DC Build Cost | Bonus Eligible | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------------------|---------------------------|---------------|------------|--------|
| Electrical distribution (switchgear, PDUs, busway, cabling) | 39-year real property | 5-7 year personal property | 15-20% | Yes (100%) | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG Ch. 7.4](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Raised floors / access flooring | 39-year real property | 5-7 year personal property | 3-5% | Yes (100%) | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Fire suppression (clean agent, VESDA) | 39-year real property | 7-year personal property | 2-4% | Yes (100%) | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Security systems (access control, CCTV, biometrics) | 39-year real property | 5-year personal property | 1-3% | Yes (100%) | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| HVAC and cooling (chillers, CRAHs, cooling towers, CDUs) | 39-year real property | 15-year personal property | 15-25% | Yes (100%) | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Generators and emergency power | 39-year real property | 15-year personal property | 5-8% | Yes (100%) | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Site work (grading, paving, fencing, landscaping) | 39-year real property | 15-year land improvement | 5-10% | Yes (100%) | confirmed | [IRS Cost Segregation ATG](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) |
| Building shell (structure, exterior walls, roof) | 39-year real property | 39-year real property | 25-35% | No | confirmed | [IRS Publication 946](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p946) |

**Aggregate impact:** A properly executed cost segregation study typically reclassifies 40-60% of total DC construction cost from 39-year to 5-15 year recovery periods. With 100% bonus depreciation, this means 40-60% of construction cost can be immediately expensed in the first year.

**Example (illustrative):**
- $500M DC construction cost
- Cost segregation reclassifies 50% ($250M) to bonus-eligible categories
- Year 1 tax deduction: $250M (immediate)
- At 21% corporate rate: ~$52.5M in first-year tax savings
- Remaining $250M (building shell): depreciated over 39 years (~$6.4M/year)

**Study requirements:** IRS expects a "quality" cost segregation study to include: engineering-based analysis (not estimate-based), detailed asset-by-asset review, site visit, proper documentation of reclassification rationale, and reference to IRS audit techniques guide methodology.

> Source: [IRS Cost Segregation Audit Techniques Guide](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/cost-segregation-audit-techniques-guide) | Confidence: confirmed

## Tax Equity Structures

Structures for monetizing federal tax credits (ITC/PTC) when the DC developer lacks sufficient tax liability to use credits directly. These structures bring in a tax equity investor who can use the credits.

**Common structures for DC on-site renewable energy and BESS projects:**

### Partnership Flip

The most common tax equity structure for renewable energy projects at data centers.

| Parameter | Typical Terms | Notes | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|--------------|-------|------------|--------|
| Structure | DC developer and tax equity investor form a partnership (LLC) | Partnership owns the on-site solar/BESS/wind asset | confirmed | [NREL Partnership Flip Model](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |
| Pre-flip allocation | Tax equity: 99% of tax benefits (ITC/PTC, depreciation, losses); DC developer: 1% | Tax equity receives nearly all tax benefits during pre-flip period | confirmed | [NREL Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |
| Flip trigger | Tax equity achieves target after-tax yield (typically 6-9% IRR, varies by market) | Flip occurs when target yield is met, typically years 5-8 | confirmed | [CohnReznick Tax Equity Guide](https://www.cohnreznick.com/) |
| Post-flip allocation | DC developer: 95%; Tax equity: 5% | DC developer gains majority control; tax equity retains nominal interest | confirmed | [NREL Partnership Flip Model](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |
| Buyout | DC developer can purchase tax equity's remaining interest at fair market value | Typically at a nominal price since most value has been extracted | confirmed | [NREL Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |

### Inverted Lease (Sale-Leaseback)

Alternative structure where the DC operator sells the energy asset to a tax equity investor and leases it back.

| Parameter | Typical Terms | Notes | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|--------------|-------|------------|--------|
| Structure | DC developer builds solar/BESS, sells to tax equity investor, leases back | Tax equity investor is the owner for tax purposes | confirmed | [NREL Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |
| Lease term | 15-25 years (matching PPA/asset life) | DC operator pays lease payments; receives energy benefit | confirmed | [NREL Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |
| Tax benefit allocation | 100% to tax equity investor (owner) | Simpler than partnership flip -- single owner claims all credits | confirmed | [NREL Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |
| Purchase option | DC operator typically has option to purchase at end of lease at fair market value | FMV purchase ensures true lease qualification for IRS | confirmed | [NREL Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |

### Transferable Tax Credits (Post-IRA / Post-OBBBA)

The IRA (2022) introduced transferability of certain tax credits, providing an alternative to traditional tax equity structures.

| Parameter | Details | Notes | Confidence | Source |
|-----------|---------|-------|------------|--------|
| Eligible credits | ITC (Sec 48/48E), PTC (Sec 45/45Y), and others | DC-relevant credits are transferable | confirmed | [IRS Transferable Credits Guidance](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/transfer-of-certain-credits) |
| Mechanism | Sell credits to unrelated taxpayer for cash | Typically sold at $0.90-$0.95 per $1.00 of credit (market pricing) | confirmed | [Crux Climate](https://www.cruxclimate.com/) |
| Advantage | Simpler than partnership flip or sale-leaseback; no complex partnership structure | Reduces transaction costs; broader buyer pool | confirmed | [IRS Transferable Credits Guidance](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/transfer-of-certain-credits) |
| Limitation | Only the credit is transferred, not depreciation or losses | Partnership flip still offers more total tax benefit (credit + depreciation) | confirmed | [NREL Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html) |

**When each structure applies:**

| Structure | Best For | DC Application | Typical Transaction Size |
|-----------|----------|---------------|------------------------|
| Partnership flip | Maximum total tax benefit (credits + depreciation) | Large on-site solar + BESS installations (>$10M) | $10M-$500M |
| Inverted lease | Simpler structure; DC operator wants to retain operational control | Mid-size installations; DC operators with limited tax structuring experience | $5M-$100M |
| Transferable credits | Quick monetization; no ongoing partnership | Any size; particularly useful for smaller installations or when tax equity market is tight | $1M-$50M |

**Post-OBBBA considerations:**
- ITC/PTC safe harbor deadline (July 4, 2026) creates urgency for tax equity commitments on new projects
- 100% bonus depreciation makes partnership flips more attractive (larger total tax benefit to allocate)
- Transferable credit market is maturing, with institutional buyers offering more competitive pricing
- Tax equity yield expectations: 6-9% after-tax IRR depending on credit type, project risk, and market conditions

> Source: [NREL Renewable Energy Tax Equity Structures](https://www.nrel.gov/analysis/tech-tax-equity.html), [IRS Transferable Credits](https://www.irs.gov/credits-deductions/transfer-of-certain-credits) | Confidence: confirmed
