## USTDA

**U.S. Trade and Development Agency**

USTDA links U.S. businesses to export opportunities by funding project preparation activities in emerging economies. Focuses on feasibility studies, technical assistance, and pilot projects that lead to the export of U.S. goods and services.

**Eligible Countries:** Emerging and developing economies across Africa, Asia, Latin America, Middle East, and Eastern Europe. Excludes high-income OECD countries and sanctioned nations.

**Typical Ticket Size:** $500K - $3M per grant (feasibility studies and technical assistance). Reverse trade missions: $50K-$150K.

**Application Process:**
1. Identify qualifying project with U.S. export potential
2. Submit project proposal through USTDA online portal
3. USTDA evaluates developmental impact and U.S. export potential
4. If approved, USTDA selects U.S. firm through competitive procurement
5. Grantee (host country entity) receives study at no cost

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- Global Connectivity Alliance -- digital infrastructure including data centers
- Clean Energy and Climate programs -- renewable-powered DC facilities
- Reverse Trade Missions -- bring emerging market buyers to US DC facilities
- Feasibility study grants for DC development in underserved markets

**Current Funding Windows:** Rolling applications; FY2026 budget emphasis on Indo-Pacific and Africa digital infrastructure.

Source: [USTDA](https://ustda.gov/)

## IFC

**International Finance Corporation (World Bank Group)**

IFC provides investment and advisory services to private sector projects in developing countries. Largest global development institution focused exclusively on the private sector. Can take equity positions, provide debt, and mobilize third-party capital.

**Eligible Countries:** IDA and IBRD member countries (140+ developing nations). Concentrated in Africa, South Asia, East Asia, Latin America, and Eastern Europe.

**Typical Ticket Size:** $10M - $200M+ per project (debt or equity). Advisory services: $500K-$5M. Blended finance with concessional tranches available.

**Application Process:**
1. Submit project concept through IFC online investment portal
2. IFC conducts preliminary screening (2-4 weeks)
3. Due diligence phase including E&S assessment (3-6 months)
4. Board approval for investments over $50M
5. Legal documentation and financial close

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- EDGE (Excellence in Design for Greater Efficiencies) certification for green buildings including data centers
- Digital infrastructure investment program targeting Africa and South Asia
- Green bond framework for climate-aligned DC projects
- InfraVentures co-development for early-stage infrastructure

**Current Funding Windows:** Continuous; FY2026 priority on digital infrastructure and climate-smart investments in Africa and South Asia.

Source: [IFC](https://www.ifc.org/)

## AfDB

**African Development Bank**

AfDB promotes economic and social development in Africa through loans, equity investments, and technical assistance. Prioritizes regional integration, private sector development, and climate resilience.

**Eligible Countries:** 54 African member states. Regional member countries receive concessional terms through African Development Fund (ADF) window.

**Typical Ticket Size:** $10M - $150M per project (sovereign or non-sovereign). Private sector window: $5M-$100M. Technical assistance grants: $200K-$2M.

**Application Process:**
1. Submit expression of interest to AfDB Private Sector Operations
2. Concept review and eligibility screening (4-6 weeks)
3. Project appraisal and due diligence (3-9 months)
4. Board presentation and approval
5. Legal negotiations and financial close

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- Africa Digital Economy initiative -- digital infrastructure investment
- Desert to Power initiative -- solar energy across the Sahel (relevant for DC+solar co-development)
- Technologies for African Agricultural Transformation -- includes digital infrastructure for agritech DC
- Partial credit guarantees to attract commercial lenders to DC projects

**Current Funding Windows:** Continuous for private sector; ADF-16 replenishment cycle active through 2028.

Source: [AfDB](https://www.afdb.org/)

## ADB

**Asian Development Bank**

ADB promotes social and economic development in Asia and the Pacific through loans, technical assistance, grants, and equity investments. Strong focus on climate finance and digital connectivity.

**Eligible Countries:** 46 developing member countries across Asia-Pacific. Includes Southeast Asia, South Asia, Central Asia, and Pacific islands.

**Typical Ticket Size:** $25M - $300M per project (sovereign or non-sovereign). Private sector: $10M-$200M. Technical assistance: $500K-$5M.

**Application Process:**
1. Submit project proposal to ADB Private Sector Operations Department
2. Initial screening and concept clearance (4-8 weeks)
3. Due diligence including environmental and social safeguards (4-8 months)
4. Board consideration for projects over $50M
5. Loan/investment agreement and effectiveness

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- Digital Technology for Development program -- includes digital infrastructure
- Asia Pacific Climate Finance Fund -- green DC development
- ASEAN Catalytic Green Finance Facility -- concessional terms for green infrastructure in SE Asia
- Venture Financing for innovative digital infrastructure

**Current Funding Windows:** Strategy 2030 midterm review active; digital infrastructure is a priority sector through FY2027.

Source: [ADB](https://www.adb.org/)

## EBRD

**European Bank for Reconstruction and Development**

EBRD invests in private enterprises and infrastructure in economies transitioning to open market systems. Covers Central and Eastern Europe, Central Asia, the Middle East, and North Africa.

**Eligible Countries:** 38 economies of operations including Poland, Romania, Turkey, Egypt, Morocco, Kazakhstan, Uzbekistan, and others in transition regions.

**Typical Ticket Size:** $5M - $250M per project. Average investment ~$25M. Can combine debt, equity, guarantees, and trade finance.

**Application Process:**
1. Contact EBRD sector team or local office with project concept
2. Concept review including transition impact assessment (2-4 weeks)
3. Due diligence and structuring (3-6 months)
4. Board approval for investments over EUR 25M
5. Signing and disbursement

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- Green Economy Transition (GET 2.1) approach -- financing for green digital infrastructure
- Property and Tourism team covers commercial real estate including DC facilities
- Infrastructure team covers power and digital connectivity
- EBRD Green Cities program -- smart city infrastructure including edge DC

**Current Funding Windows:** GET 2.1 target: 50%+ of investments in green economy by 2025. Digital infrastructure is a priority sub-sector.

Source: [EBRD](https://www.ebrd.com/)

## Power Africa

**U.S. Government Power Africa Initiative**

Power Africa is a U.S. government-led partnership coordinating investment in African energy infrastructure. Works through multiple U.S. agencies (USAID, USTDA, OPIC/DFC, MCC) and private sector partners.

**Eligible Countries:** Sub-Saharan African nations. Priority countries include Nigeria, Kenya, Ethiopia, Tanzania, Ghana, South Africa, Senegal, and Cote d'Ivoire.

**Typical Ticket Size:** Transaction advisory (no cost to developer); mobilizes $5M-$500M+ through partner institutions (DFC, IFC, AfDB). Direct grant programs: $100K-$5M for technical assistance.

**Application Process:**
1. Contact Power Africa through USAID mission or online portal
2. Transaction advisory team evaluates project fit
3. Power Africa connects developer with appropriate financing partners (DFC, USTDA, IFC, commercial banks)
4. Ongoing transaction support through financial close
5. Post-close monitoring and technical assistance

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- Beyond the Grid initiative -- off-grid and distributed power relevant to remote DC
- Health Electrification program -- demonstrates template for critical facility power
- Clean Energy Transaction Advisory -- solar/wind procurement for DC power
- Connects developer to DFC (formerly OPIC) for debt/equity financing up to $300M

**Current Funding Windows:** Continuous transaction advisory; Power Africa targets 30GW of new generation by 2030.

Source: [Power Africa](https://www.usaid.gov/powerafrica)

## GCF

**Green Climate Fund**

GCF is the world's largest dedicated climate fund, established under the UNFCCC to support developing countries in climate mitigation and adaptation. Provides grants, loans, equity, and guarantees.

**Eligible Countries:** All developing country parties to the UNFCCC (over 150 countries). Prioritizes Least Developed Countries (LDCs), Small Island Developing States (SIDS), and African states.

**Typical Ticket Size:** $10M - $500M per project (through Accredited Entities). Simplified approval for projects under $10M. Readiness grants: $300K-$3M.

**Application Process:**
1. Identify Accredited Entity (AE) to submit proposal (national or international)
2. Develop Funding Proposal with AE following GCF templates
3. Independent Technical Advisory Panel review
4. GCF Board approval (meets 3 times/year)
5. Funded Activity Agreement and disbursement

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- Mitigation: renewable energy-powered DC projects reducing grid emissions
- Cross-cutting: digital infrastructure enabling climate adaptation (weather monitoring, disaster response)
- Private Sector Facility -- direct engagement with DC developers for green facilities
- Technology transfer: energy-efficient cooling and power management systems

**Current Funding Windows:** Second replenishment (GCF-2) active 2024-2027; emphasis on transformational projects in LDCs and SIDS.

Source: [GCF](https://www.greenclimate.fund/)

## MIGA

**Multilateral Investment Guarantee Agency (World Bank Group)**

MIGA provides political risk insurance (guarantees) for cross-border investments in developing countries. Does not provide direct financing but de-risks investment to attract private capital.

**Eligible Countries:** MIGA member developing countries (over 150 nations). Investor must be from a different member country than the host.

**Typical Ticket Size:** $5M - $250M per guarantee. Maximum coverage up to $250M per project; can be increased to $500M+ with reinsurance. Typical premium: 0.5-1.5% of coverage.

**Application Process:**
1. Submit Definitive Application through MIGA online portal (preliminary application optional)
2. MIGA conducts underwriting assessment including political risk evaluation (2-4 months)
3. Environmental and social review per MIGA Performance Standards
4. Contract of Guarantee issuance
5. Coverage effective for up to 15 years (extendable to 20)

**DC-Relevant Programs:**
- Political risk coverage: expropriation, war/civil disturbance, breach of contract, currency transfer restriction
- Small Investment Program (SIP) for projects under $10M with streamlined processing
- Covers equity, debt, and non-shareholder loans for DC projects in developing countries
- Particularly valuable for DC investments in countries with sovereign risk concerns

**Current Funding Windows:** Continuous applications; FY2026 emphasis on climate, digital infrastructure, and fragile/conflict-affected states.

Source: [MIGA](https://www.miga.org/)
