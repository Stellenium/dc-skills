**Cost drivers:**
- AI-ready premium: +50-100% over traditional builds due to liquid cooling infrastructure, higher power density electrical, and reinforced structural requirements
- Land: $50K-$500K/acre depending on market (NoVA at premium, rural TX/AZ at low end)
- Permitting timeline: 6-18 months adds carrying costs; fast-track markets (TX, AZ) save 3-6 months
- Grid interconnection: $500K-$15M depending on capacity and utility (not included in $/MW figures above)

> Note: All figures in USD. $/MW refers to total project cost per megawatt of IT load capacity (not electrical capacity). Original sources may report in $/W or $/kW -- converted to $/MW for consistency per project convention.

## Europe Construction Costs

European data center construction costs generally track higher than US due to labor costs, regulatory requirements, and land constraints in core markets. All figures converted to USD at approximately 1 EUR = 1.08 USD (March 2026).

| Dublin | $11.0-13.5M | Grid moratorium easing; renewable energy requirements | confirmed | [Turner & Townsend 2025-2026](https://www.turnerandtownsend.com/insights/data-centre-construction-cost-index-2025-2026/) |
| Nordics (Stockholm, Helsinki) | $9.5-12.0M | Lower cooling costs offset by logistics; abundant hydro/wind | confirmed | [Turner & Townsend 2025-2026](https://www.turnerandtownsend.com/insights/data-centre-construction-cost-index-2025-2026/) |

| Madrid/Milan | $10.0-12.5M | Emerging Southern European markets | estimated | Industry estimates |

**European cost factors:**
- Energy Performance of Buildings Directive (EPBD): requires PUE < 1.3 for new builds (from 2027) -- adds ~5-10% to M&E costs
- EU Taxonomy alignment for sustainable finance may be required for institutional investors
- VAT applies to construction (17-27% depending on country; excluded from figures above)
- Prevailing wage requirements in most EU markets increase labor costs 15-30% vs US non-union markets

**APAC-specific cost factors:**
- Equipment import duties: 5-20% in India, Indonesia, Malaysia (not applicable in Singapore free trade zones)
- Seismic design: +10-15% in Tokyo, Osaka, Taipei for earthquake-resistant structures
- Cooling advantage: Singapore and tropical markets require year-round cooling (no free cooling); Nordic-style savings not available
- Land scarcity: Singapore, Tokyo, Hong Kong drive vertical construction (multi-story DC), adding 20-30% to structural costs

**Power cost considerations:**
- Demand charges: $5-15/kW/month in most US markets; can add 1-3 cents/kWh effective rate
- Ratchet clauses: Peak demand sets minimum billing for 6-12 months in many utility tariffs
- Renewable energy: PPAs increasingly cheaper than grid in solar/wind-rich markets (TX, IA, Nordics)
- Behind-the-meter generation: On-site solar + BESS can reduce effective rate by 20-40% in favorable markets

**Labor market trends (2025-2026):**
- Skilled electrician shortage: 2-4 month lead times for large project staffing in top markets
- Traveling workforce: 15-25% premium for importing skilled trades to secondary markets
- Modular/prefab: Reduces on-site labor requirements by 30-40%; shifts cost to factory labor (typically lower-cost markets)
- AI/HPC specialty premium: Liquid cooling installation labor commands 20-30% premium over traditional HVAC trades
- Prevailing wage markets (VA, OR, IL, NY) add 15-35% to labor costs vs right-to-work states (TX, AZ, GA)

> Sources: [Bureau of Labor Statistics OES](https://www.bls.gov/oes/), [Turner & Townsend Cost Index](https://www.turnerandtownsend.com/insights/data-centre-construction-cost-index-2025-2026/), industry project data.
