# US State Data Center Tax Incentives

> Sales tax, property tax, and other incentive programs for data center
> investment across 42 US states. Organized by region for selective loading.
> Skills load individual sections by H2 header -- do not load the entire file.
>
> NOTE: State incentive programs change rapidly. Over 300 bills affecting
> data center incentives were filed in 2026 alone. Always verify current
> status with state revenue departments before relying on this data.

## Northeast States

### Virginia

- **Incentive:** Sales and use tax exemption on computer equipment and enabling software for data centers investing $150M+ and creating 50+ jobs (or $150M+ in high-cost areas with 25+ jobs)
- **Threshold:** $150M capital investment; 50 jobs (standard) or 25 jobs (high-cost localities)
- **Sunset:** No sunset on existing program
- **Clawback:** Must maintain investment and job thresholds for duration of exemption period; annual reporting to Virginia Economic Development Partnership required
- **Annual cost:** ~$1.6B in foregone revenue (estimated, 2025 fiscal year) -- largest state DC incentive program in the US
- **Status:** Active but under significant legislative review
- **Pending:** Multiple 2026 bills propose caps on annual exemption amounts, moratoriums on new approvals, and increased community benefit requirements. HB 2066 and SB 1078 are lead bills.
- **Confidence:** confirmed (existing program); unconfirmed (pending legislation outcomes)
- **Source:** [Virginia Code Section 58.1-609.3(18)](https://law.lis.virginia.gov/vacode/title58.1/chapter6/section58.1-609.3/), [MultiState Feb 2026](https://www.multistate.us/insider/2026/2/4/states-rethink-data-center-tax-incentives-as-costs-soar)

### New York

- **Incentive:** Excelsior Jobs Program -- tax credits for job creation and investment in data centers; Empire State Development regional incentives
- **Threshold:** Varies by region; minimum job creation requirements
- **Sunset:** Excelsior program extended through 2039
- **Clawback:** Job creation and retention targets; clawback of credits if targets not met within 3 years
- **Annual cost:** Not separately reported for DC sector
- **Status:** Active
- **Pending:** None significant in 2026
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [Empire State Development](https://esd.ny.gov/excelsior-jobs-program)

## Southeast States

### Georgia

- **Incentive:** Sales and use tax exemption on data center equipment for facilities investing $150M+ or purchasing $5M+ annually in electricity; high-tech tax credit
- **Threshold:** $150M capital investment (standard); $100M+ and 50+ jobs (high-tech credit)
- **Sunset:** Exemption extended through 2031 (previously 2028)
- **Clawback:** Investment and electricity consumption thresholds; annual certification
- **Annual cost:** ~$200M+ (estimated, growing rapidly)
- **Status:** Active but facing repeal pressure similar to Virginia
- **Pending:** 2026 legislation proposing caps on annual exemptions and community impact requirements; HB 1043 proposes moratorium on new approvals
- **Confidence:** confirmed (existing program); unconfirmed (pending legislation)
- **Source:** [Georgia Code Section 48-8-3(68)](https://law.justia.com/codes/georgia/title-48/), [MultiState Feb 2026](https://www.multistate.us/insider/2026/2/4/states-rethink-data-center-tax-incentives-as-costs-soar)

- **Clawback:** Annual reporting; job and investment maintenance
- **Status:** Active
- **Pending:** None significant
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [NC Commerce](https://www.commerce.nc.gov/)

### Louisiana

- **Incentive:** Enterprise Zone tax credits; Industrial Tax Exemption Program (ITEP) -- property tax exemption for manufacturing (DC equipment qualifies); Quality Jobs Program
- **Threshold:** ITEP: no minimum (manufacturing); Quality Jobs: 5+ jobs, $1M+ annual payroll
- **Sunset:** ITEP reformed in 2016 (Board of Commerce and Industry approval required); Quality Jobs ongoing
- **Clawback:** Job and investment commitments per program
- **Annual cost:** ITEP statewide ~$1.5B (all industries)

## Southwest States

### Texas

- **Incentive:** Chapter 313 successor program -- Texas Jobs, Energy, Technology, and Innovation Act (JETI, HB 5, 2023) provides property tax value limitations for qualifying projects; no state income tax; local property tax abatements (Chapter 312/313 successor)
- **Threshold:** JETI: varies by school district; projects must demonstrate economic benefit; minimum $200M for large-scale DC
- **Sunset:** JETI program authorized through 2033; individual agreements typically 10 years
- **Clawback:** Job creation and investment maintenance; annual reporting to Comptroller
- **Annual cost:** Former Chapter 313 cost ~$1.1B/year across all industries
- **Status:** Active (JETI replaced Chapter 313 which expired Dec 31, 2022)
- **Pending:** Proposals to expand JETI eligibility and extend program duration
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [Texas Comptroller](https://comptroller.texas.gov/economy/local/ch313/), [Texas Legislature HB 5](https://capitol.texas.gov/BillLookup/History.aspx?LegSess=88R&Bill=HB5)

- **Sunset:** Sales tax exemption: through 2033 (extended)
- **Clawback:** Investment and job compliance over 10-year period
- **Status:** Active
- **Pending:** Proposals to increase community benefit requirements
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [Arizona Commerce](https://www.azcommerce.com/)

### New Mexico

- **Threshold:** High-wage credit: jobs paying above certain thresholds; IRBs: negotiated with local government

- **Clawback:** Job wage requirements; IRB agreement terms
- **Status:** Active (general incentives, not DC-specific)
- **Pending:** Proposed dedicated DC incentive legislation in 2026
- **Confidence:** confirmed (existing); unconfirmed (proposed)
- **Source:** [NM EDD](https://edd.newmexico.gov/)

### Oklahoma

- **Threshold:** $50M investment; 5+ jobs; average wage of $68K+
- **Sunset:** Through 2026 (expiring soon)
- **Clawback:** Job and investment maintenance
- **Status:** Active but facing repeal -- significant 2026 legislative efforts to eliminate or reduce DC incentives
- **Pending:** HB 3400 and SB 1300 propose repealing DC sales tax exemptions; counter-proposals to extend with higher thresholds
- **Confidence:** confirmed (current program); unconfirmed (repeal outcome)

## West States

### Washington

- **Incentive:** Data center sales and use tax exemption (RCW 82.08.986) on server equipment, power infrastructure, and cooling equipment for rural county data centers
- **Threshold:** Facility in eligible rural county; no minimum investment for sales tax exemption
- **Sunset:** Through 2027 (one of the oldest DC incentive programs, originally enacted 2010)
- **Clawback:** Must maintain facility in eligible county
- **Status:** Active
- **Pending:** Extension discussions expected in 2027 session
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [WA DOR](https://dor.wa.gov/), [RCW 82.08.986](https://app.leg.wa.gov/rcw/default.aspx?cite=82.08.986)

### Oregon

- **Incentive:** Enterprise Zone property tax exemption (3-5 years standard; up to 15 years for large projects); no sales tax statewide; Strategic Investment Program (SIP) for projects $100M+
- **Threshold:** Enterprise Zone: varies by zone; SIP: $100M+ investment and community service fee
- **Sunset:** Enterprise Zones: per designation; SIP: 15-year property tax abatement cap
- **Clawback:** Enterprise Zone: job creation commitments; SIP: community service fee payments
- **Status:** Active (Oregon is a top 5 DC market due to no sales tax + cheap hydropower)
- **Pending:** None significant
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [Business Oregon](https://www.oregon.gov/biz/)

### California

- **Incentive:** No dedicated DC incentive program; California Competes Tax Credit (negotiated); Governor's Office of Business and Economic Development (GO-Biz) assistance
- **Threshold:** CalCompetes: negotiated per project; competitive application
- **Sunset:** CalCompetes: annual appropriation
- **Clawback:** Per agreement terms; job creation milestones
- **Status:** Active (limited -- California is a challenging DC incentive environment due to high costs and strict environmental review)
- **Pending:** None significant for DC-specific incentives
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [GO-Biz California](https://business.ca.gov/)

### Nevada

- **Threshold:** $25M investment; 10+ jobs (standard); $100M+ for enhanced 20-year abatement
- **Sunset:** Standard: 10-20 years per agreement; Enhanced: 20 years
- **Clawback:** Investment and job maintenance; annual reporting to Governor's Office of Economic Development
- **Status:** Active (Nevada is a top 5 DC market, particularly Reno and Las Vegas)
- **Pending:** None significant
- **Confidence:** confirmed
- **Source:** [GOED Nevada](https://goed.nv.gov/), [NRS 360.754](https://www.leg.state.nv.us/nrs/nrs-360.html)

### Utah
